## Player Archetypes

### Archetype 1: The Tribal Fan
**Who they are:** Deeply invested in one national team. Watches every match their team plays. Likely also follows club football.
**World Cup behavior:** Consumes their team's coverage obsessively. Participates in fan forums. Has strong opinions on selections, formations, refereeing.
**What they want from a game:** A way to express and signal depth of knowledge and loyalty. Validation that their opinions are shared (or interestingly divergent). Status among other fans of the same nation.
**What they don't want:** To be lumped with casual fans who "just support whoever."
**Gap-day behavior:** Deep-dives on tactical analysis, transfer rumors, injury updates for their team.
**Mechanic fit:** Seeds 3, 4 (Rival Reading / Reputation Market) — gives them a platform for strong opinions without requiring prediction.

---

### Archetype 2: The Neutral Adopter
**Who they are:** Watches the World Cup as a cultural event. No strong national allegiance or supporting a team that was eliminated early. Documented behavior: adopted Morocco in 2022 having never watched Morocco before.
**World Cup behavior:** Follows the narrative, not the football. Cares about the underdog story, the villain team, the breakout player. Watches big matches. Stops watching if their adopted team exits.
**What they want from a game:** A reason to pick a side and feel something. Social coordination with friends. A way to participate in the event even without football knowledge.
**What they don't want:** To feel dumb because they don't know who plays at left back.
**Gap-day behavior:** Consumes reaction content, memes, player discovery content. Decides whether to adopt a new team after elimination.
**Key insight:** This is the growth market. Drive to Survive created this person for F1. Copa must work for this person — or it does not scale.
**Mechanic fit:** Seeds B, D (Crowd Read / Interpretation Gap) — football knowledge is irrelevant, social modeling ability is the skill.

---

### Archetype 3: The Group Chat Organizer
**Who they are:** The person in friend group or family group who sends the first match-day meme, starts the viewing party conversation, shares the highlight clip.
**World Cup behavior:** Not necessarily the deepest football fan — but the social catalyst. Has a WhatsApp group or Discord server specifically for the tournament.
**What they want from a game:** Something they can share to the group. A conversation starter, not a leaderboard. A mechanic that works for the full group including non-football people.
**What they don't want:** To be the only person who cares. To have to explain complex rules to get friends involved.
**Gap-day behavior:** Maintains the group chat between matches. Drops content to keep people engaged.
**Key insight:** This person is Copa's primary distribution channel AND primary monetization target (Jackbox model — one buyer per group). The game must be shareable *by* this person *to* non-players who immediately understand what it is. Copa's shareable artifact is designed for this person to drop into a group chat.
**Mechanic fit:** All seeds — but specifically needs a one-tap shareable artifact.

---

### Archetype 4: The Diaspora Fan
**Who they are:** Second or third-generation immigrant. Has complex national allegiance — may support both their birth country and ancestral country.
**World Cup behavior:** Re-engages with ancestral national identity during the tournament. Follows news in both languages. Watches with family in ways they don't watch club football.
**What they want from a game:** A way to express layered identity. Not forced to choose one team. A game that treats national identity as complex, not just a flag.
**What they don't want:** A game that doesn't recognize their team or treats their nation as irrelevant.
**Gap-day behavior:** Cultural content discovery. Player backstory consumption (especially players with their diaspora background).
**Mechanic fit:** Seed E (Streak Correspondent) — assigned nation mechanic could assign them their ancestral nation; gives structure to the identity re-engagement they already experience.

---

### Archetype 5: The Opinion Machine
**Who they are:** Has strong takes on everything. May or may not know football. Immediately forms views on refereeing decisions, player behavior, team tactics. Already arguing in group chats.
**World Cup behavior:** Live-texts or group-chats during matches. Has a villain team within the first week. Relitigates controversial calls for days.
**What they want from a game:** Somewhere to register their opinions and see if others agree or disagree. Validation or the satisfaction of being contrarian.
**What they don't want:** To be evaluated on football knowledge. To be wrong in a way that feels like stupidity rather than opinion.
**Gap-day behavior:** Argument maintenance. "Was that really offside?" content consumption.
**Mechanic fit:** Seeds 1, 3, 5, D (Narrative Tribunal / Rival Reading / Attribution Game / Interpretation Gap) — opinion expression without knowledge requirement.

---

### Archetype 6: The Social Modeler (New — Identified Gen 3)
**Who they are:** Drawn to games where the skill is understanding other people, not domain knowledge. Would do well at Family Feud, Herd Mentality, Scruples. Not necessarily a football fan at all.
**World Cup behavior:** Participates because of the social occasion, not the football. Engaged by questions about what "people" think more than questions about what "happened."
**What they want from a game:** A mechanic where their skill (reading social dynamics) is as valid as football knowledge. A game they can be good at without watching matches closely.
**What they don't want:** To be at a permanent disadvantage because they can't identify an offside trap.
**Gap-day behavior:** Social media interpretation. Following the narrative through reactions and memes rather than match replays.
**Key insight:** This archetype is unlocked specifically by the Crowd Read mechanic (Seed B). No existing sports game addresses this archetype. They are the growth market that Copa could uniquely capture.
**Mechanic fit:** Seed B (Crowd Read) is designed for this person. They may outperform football experts.
