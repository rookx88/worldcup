# Target Players

*Profiles of the people this game is built for.*
*Copa will develop these across generations based on research.*

---

## Player 1: The Casual Watcher — "Sofia"

**Archetype:** Watches the big World Cup matches (quarters, semis, final, her country's games). Doesn't follow club football. Knows Mbappe and maybe 5 other names.

**What she does on match day:**
Texts her friends predictions before kickoff. "I think Brazil wins but Morocco has an upset in them." Watches with a group. Reacts emotionally to moments — goals, red cards, penalties. Forgets the score by the next day unless her team was involved.

**Why existing fantasy games don't work for her:**
DraftKings requires knowing player salaries and stats — she doesn't know who plays left back for Portugal. FIFA Fantasy requires building a roster of players she's never heard of. FPL is club football. All of them feel like homework.

**What would make her sign up:**
A game where she can make her predictions in 2 minutes before kickoff, using names she recognizes (or a shortlist that shows her faces/numbers), and then see a visual result after the match that shows how right she was.

**What would make her share:**
Her card showing she called the upset. A graphic that says "Sofia called Morocco 2–1 Spain" with a big green checkmark. She'd post that to her Instagram story immediately. She already posts about the World Cup — this gives her something more concrete to post.

**Acquisition channel:** Instagram, TikTok, friend referral, co-watching groups.

## Player 2: The Competitive Fan — "Marcus"

**Archetype:** Watches most Premier League matches, plays FPL religiously, knows squad depths and injury news. Already plays DFS occasionally but finds soccer DFS less satisfying than NFL.

**What he does on match day:**
Checks injury reports and lineup confirmations. Has strong opinions about who's going to score. Monitors his FPL/DFS lineup until kickoff. Watches the match partly as validation of his picks.

**Why existing fantasy games don't work for him (at the World Cup):**
DraftKings soccer DFS has low liquidity and bad contest variety during the World Cup. FIFA Official Fantasy is too passive — no real decisions during the tournament. He wants something with edge and competition, but the existing World Cup options don't scratch that itch.

**What would make him sign up:**
The ability to compete in a private league against people he knows. The feeling that his knowledge gives him an edge — the goalscorer shortlist should include obscure correct picks that reward knowing the squad. Leaderboards, streaks, "bold call" recognition.

**What would make him share:**
Ranking #1 in his friend group. Getting a "bold call confirmed" notification when his obscure pick scores. Having a stat like "Marcus is 7/9 on goalscorer predictions."

**Acquisition channel:** r/FantasyPL, Twitter/X soccer community, friend referral from Sofia types.

## Player 3: The Group Organizer — "Javier"

**Archetype:** The person in the friend group who sets up the bracket challenge, organizes the watch party, texts everyone to make their picks. Mid-level soccer fan — follows his national team (Mexico or USMNT given 2026 host country angle) closely but not club football globally.

**What he does on match day:**
Sends the group chat "who's everyone picking?" before the match. Keeps track of who's been right. Takes pride in running the competition. Probably already uses a Google Form or group chat polls for predictions.

**Why existing fantasy games don't work for him:**
He can't get everyone in his group to sign up for DraftKings — too complex, requires deposits for paid contests. FIFA Fantasy loses people after the first week because there's no social hook. He needs something all 8–12 people in his group will actually use, including the ones who don't follow football closely.

**What would make him sign up:**
A private group league where he can invite friends with a simple link. The ability to see the whole group's cards in one view. Something that generates conversation — "Javier called it, Maria got it completely wrong."

**What would make him share:**
Sharing the group invite link. Posting the group leaderboard at the end of the tournament. Bragging rights are the product for him.

**Acquisition channel:** Direct outreach in WhatsApp/group chat by him. He is Copa's most valuable growth vector — one Javier brings 8–12 players.

---

## Primary Target (V1 Focus)
**Sofia** — she defines the product decisions. If Sofia can use it, Marcus and Javier can definitely use it. Building for Marcus first produces something Sofia won't sign up for.
