## How Comparable Viral Games Actually Monetize — Evidence

### GeoGuessr (Most Relevant Freemium Case)
- Free tier: 5 games/month (restricted from unlimited in 2022)
- Pro tier: $24/year (~600K subscribers estimated)
- Estimated ARR: ~$15M on base of ~40M registered users
- Conversion rate: ~1.5% of registered users to paid
- Key lesson: Restricting the free tier after building a free user base drove significant conversion. Players who played free for years converted when the limit hit.
- **What Copa takes from this:** Build free users first. Freemium restriction is a later-stage lever, not a launch lever.

### Immaculate Grid
- Free to play, ad-supported
- 500K daily users within weeks of launch
- Revenue: display advertising
- No paid tier
- **What Copa takes from this:** Display ads are viable at 500K+ daily users. Not viable at launch. Ad-supported is a scale model, not a seed model.

### Jackbox Games (Most Applicable Model)
- Price: $25-40 per party pack. $8-12 per individual game.
- Model: One buyer per group. 6-8 players play free on phones using a shared code.
- The buyer is always the Group Chat Organizer archetype.
- Zero-budget distribution: Twitch streamers played Jackbox with audiences. Product designed to be watched.
- **What Copa takes from this:** The monetization target is not every player. It is one buyer per social group. The Group Chat Organizer pays; friends join free. This is structurally compatible with Copa's viral loop — the game must spread through group chats, and the organizer of that group is the natural payer.

### Wordle / Heardle / Flagle (What Not to Do)
- Wordle: Sold to NYT for ~$1M. Was free. No monetization model. Acquirable asset, not a business.
- Heardle: Acquired by Spotify. Shut down when Spotify deprioritized it. Strategic asset, not standalone revenue.
- Flagle: ~200K daily users, ~$2K/month Patreon at 0.1% conversion rate. Passion project numbers.
- **What Copa takes from this:** Building viral without monetization is building an asset to sell, not a business. Patreon at 0.1% conversion is survivable only as a hobby. Copa needs a real model from day one.

### Sports Media Sponsored Moments (Precedent)
- MLB Statcast Presented by Google — data feature with brand attached
- Sky Sports Goal of the Month (sponsored) — audience votes on real content with brand integration
- Mastercard Priceless Moments — real sports moments branded retroactively
- **What Copa takes from this:** If Copa creates a daily "moment" product (the Copa Moment from Match 24), that moment can be "presented by" a sponsor. No disruption to gameplay. Scales with cultural salience of moments chosen.

## Copa Monetization Stack (Evidence-Based)

### Layer 1: Free Daily Play (Acquisition Layer)
Core mechanic is always free. No paywall on the primary game loop. This is non-negotiable — it is how viral daily games grow.

### Layer 2: Group Hosting Mode (Jackbox Model)
- One Group Chat Organizer per friend group pays to host a Copa session.
- Friends join by link or code — always free.
- Price point: $5-10 one-time purchase per World Cup (not subscription — removes friction).
- What the host gets: A shareable group results page, visible to all members. Group leaderboard. Ability to set group-specific prompts or themes.
- What friends get free: Full participation in all Copa mechanics.
- **Why this works:** The organizer is already motivated to pay for the group experience. Jackbox proved one buyer per group is viable. The World Cup's natural group watching behavior means every friend group has a natural organizer.

### Layer 3: Sponsored Moments
- Each Copa Canon moment, Crowd Read result, or Reputation shift can be "presented by" a brand.
- Format: "Today's Copa Moment, presented by [Brand]" — one sponsor per match day.
- No gameplay disruption. Brand association with emotionally resonant content.
- Revenue scales with Copa's daily active users and the cultural salience of the tournament.
- **Timing:** Available from tournament launch. Does not require a large user base to approach sponsors — approach with the narrative (Copa is the game that lets fans write the tournament's story).

### Layer 4: Archive Tier (Post-Tournament)
- Free users: access to current tournament only.
- Paid subscribers (~$5/month or $15 for tournament duration): full history of how crowd opinion evolved on specific moments, reputation timelines, personal Canon history.
- Target buyer: the engaged fan who wants to revisit and share the story of the tournament.
- Also viable as a data product for football media — journalists and analysts pay for aggregate fan opinion data.

### Layer 5: Display Advertising (Scale Threshold)
- Only viable at 50K+ daily active users.
- Simple display ads or promoted content.
- Not a launch model. A scale model activated once traffic thresholds are reached.

## V1 Monetization (Pre-Launch Simplification)
For V1 (before June 18, 2026), only Layer 2 and Layer 3 are in scope:
- **Group Hosting:** One buyer per group. Manual implementation acceptable at V1 scale — email-based access codes, Stripe payment link. No backend required.
- **Sponsored Moments:** Approach 2-3 brands with a sponsor package pre-launch. First mover advantage. Package: "Copa Canon Presenting Sponsor" — brand name on the daily moment reveal for the duration of the group stage.

All other layers activate post-launch based on traction.

## Monetization Anti-Patterns (Evidence-Based)
1. **Paywalling the core mechanic at launch.** Every successful viral game grew free first. Paywalling kills the viral loop before it starts.
2. **Subscription from day one.** Subscription friction is too high without established habit. One-time purchase (Jackbox model) is the right V1 price structure.
3. **Ad-supported at launch with no user base.** Display ads are not meaningful revenue at sub-50K users. They add friction without meaningful return.
4. **Betting on acquisition.** Copa has zero budget. Any monetization model that requires paying for users before generating revenue is not viable.
