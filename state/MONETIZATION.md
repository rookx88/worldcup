# Monetization

*How Copa makes money. This is a first-class concern — not a Phase 2 afterthought.*

## Constraint
- Zero budget for paid acquisition — monetization must be built into the product, not dependent on ad spend
- Must be live before or at tournament start (June 18, 2026)
- Must not break the core experience for free players — the game must be fun and shareable for free users or growth dies

## Models Under Evaluation

### 1. Premium Group Leagues ⭐ (Frontrunner)
**How it works:** Creating a group league is free up to 6 players. Groups of 7+ require a one-time payment (~$5–10) from the organizer. Organizer gets a "Pro League" badge and additional features (custom league name, end-of-tournament trophy card, custom invite page).

**Why this works:**
- Javier (the Group Organizer archetype) is the natural buyer — he's already used to paying for office pools, bracket challenges, etc.
- One Javier = 7–12 players who all play for free → one paid conversion drives organic growth
- Low friction: $5–10 for a 6-week tournament is easy to justify
- No payment required from casual players — growth isn't blocked

**Revenue math:** 1,000 group organizers × $7 avg = $7,000 in tournament window. Achievable.

### 2. Prediction Power-Ups
**How it works:** Each player gets standard picks for free. A "Copa Pro" weekly pass ($3/week or $10 for full tournament) unlocks:
- One extra "insurance" pick per match (change your result pick up to 5 mins after kickoff)
- Access to "Bold Call Challenge" — a bonus prediction with 5× scoring for subscribers only
- Streak protection (one miss doesn't break your streak)

**Why this works:**
- Competitive players (Marcus archetype) will pay for edge
- Recurring revenue during the tournament window
- Doesn't disadvantage free players in fun — just gives competitive players more tools

**Revenue math:** 500 subscribers × $10 tournament pass = $5,000. Conservative.

### 3. Cosmetic Copa Cards
**How it works:** Base card is free (standard dark navy design). Premium card skins available ($1.99 each or included in Copa Pro):
- "Golden Boot" skin — gold theme, animated shimmer when correct
- National team themes — your card styled in your team's colors
- "Legendary" skin — unlocked only after a Perfect Card (creates FOMO and aspiration)

**Why this works:**
- Zero gameplay impact — doesn't affect fairness
- Sofia (the casual fan) shares her card publicly — premium cards look better on Instagram
- Aspirational unlocks create engagement loops independent of monetization

**Revenue math:** Hard to estimate alone, but natural upsell inside Copa Pro.

### 4. Sponsored Match Moments
**How it works:** Brands sponsor specific pick categories. Example: "The VAR Decision, presented by [insurance brand]" or "First Goalscorer, presented by [sports drink]."

**Why this works:**
- Zero cost to players
- Natural brand fit (sports betting adjacent brands, sports drinks, streaming services broadcasting WC)
- Can be approached for pre-launch sponsorship deals once validation post lands

**Revenue math:** Highly variable, but one mid-tier sponsor = $2,000–10,000 for tournament window.

### 5. Sportsbook Affiliate
**How it works:** "Want to bet on your picks? [Partner] has the best lines." Opt-in affiliate link shown after card resolves. Revenue per referred depositing user (~$30–100 CPA depending on jurisdiction).

**Why this works:**
- 100% passive once set up
- Users who made bold picks are primed to want to "back it with money" after seeing their card
- Legal in US if clearly disclosed and not integrated into the game itself

**Risk:** Requires partnership. Must be disclosed. Some platforms ban affiliate links. Design as an opt-in, never a dark pattern.

---

## Decision Framework (Copa to resolve by Gen 4)

Copa should pick a **primary** and one **secondary** monetization model before designing further. The primary model shapes the product — e.g., if Premium Group Leagues is primary, every feature should make group play more social and compelling.

**Recommendation to evaluate:** Premium Group Leagues (primary) + Copa Pro Pass (secondary).

These two stack naturally: Javier buys the group upgrade, Marcus buys the pro pass, Sofia plays free and generates the shares that bring both of them in.
