# Skill: Research

You are researching the market to find the game mechanic Copa should build. This is not competitor analysis — it is inspiration hunting across games, social apps, and entertainment.

## What You Must NOT Do
- Do not analyze DraftKings, FanDuel, FIFA Fantasy, or any existing fantasy/prediction product. That space is exhausted.
- Do not propose a mechanic during this cycle. Research only. The mechanic comes after the evidence.
- Do not return with vague statements like "casual fans want something simple." Find specific products, specific data, specific behaviors.

## What You Must Research

### Area 1: Viral Daily Games (2022–2026)
Document 5-8 games that spread virally with zero paid advertising:
- Wordle, NYT Connections, Heardle, GeoGuessr, Immaculate Grid, Globle, Poeltl, Framed, Worldle
- For each: What is the exact mechanic? What triggers sharing? What creates the daily return habit? What is the one "hook" that explains its spread?
- Look for the **pattern** across all of them — what do viral daily games have in common that traditional games don't?

### Area 2: Group Social Games
Document 4-6 games that work in a group chat or party setting with mixed skill levels:
- skribbl.io, Gartic Phone, Jackbox Party Pack, Among Us, GeoGuessr Battle Royale, Kahoot
- For each: What does the group dynamic add that solo play doesn't? What creates the memorable "moment" that people talk about afterward?
- What behavior do people already exhibit in World Cup group chats that a game could formalize?

### Area 3: Non-Prediction Sports Games
Document any sports-adjacent products that are growing and are NOT fantasy/prediction:
- What do people do during the Super Bowl on their phones that isn't DFS?
- What is the "second screen" behavior during the World Cup that already happens organically?
- Is there evidence of any game mechanic that worked during a live sporting event?

### Area 4: Role / Allegiance Games
Document how games use identity, allegiance, or role assignment:
- How does national identity create engagement differently from just "picking a team"?
- What games assign players a role or faction and use that as a mechanic?
- What does having a "side" you didn't choose do to engagement vs. a side you picked?

### Area 5: What People Do Between Matches
The World Cup has 1-3 day gaps between matches. Document:
- What content do fans consume between matches? (YouTube, TikTok, forums, podcasts)
- What games or activities spike in fan communities between matches vs. during matches?
- Is there a gap-day behavior no product has addressed?

## What to Look For
The goal is to find a **mechanic from outside sports** that the World Cup's unique properties make possible. Specifically:
- A mechanic that requires the crowd (not just the individual player)
- A mechanic that uses national allegiance as something more than a cosmetic skin
- A mechanic that creates memorable social moments between specific friends or group members
- A mechanic where knowing nothing about football is fine — or even an advantage

## Output
Write your findings to CONCEPT.md as a **Research Report** — not a concept, just evidence:
- What you found in each area
- Specific products, specific data, specific behaviors
- The patterns that emerged across areas
- 3-5 "mechanic seeds" — raw observations that could become a game, not yet developed

Update PLAYERS.md with any player archetypes that emerged from research.
Update CHANNELS.md with any community findings.
Document everything in JOURNAL.md.

## Do Not
- Name a final concept this cycle
- Design scoring systems
- Spec technical implementation
- Propose actions

Research only. The design cycle comes after.
