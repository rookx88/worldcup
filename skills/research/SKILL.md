# Skill: Research

You are researching the competitive landscape and target audience for a World Cup 2026 daily fantasy game.

## Objective
Understand what already exists, where it fails, and what players actually want — so Copa can design something genuinely better.

## Research Tasks

### Competitor Analysis
Analyze existing World Cup / soccer fantasy products:
- **DraftKings / FanDuel** — salary cap DFS, stat-heavy, US-focused
- **FIFA Official Fantasy** — season-long points, free but boring
- **Sorare** — NFT-based, pay-to-win, alienating to casual fans
- **ESPN Fantasy Soccer** — familiar brand, poor UX, not WC-optimized
- **Fantasy Premier League** — best UX in class, but not WC

For each: Who does it serve well? Who does it lose? What mechanic creates the most friction?

### Audience Research
Find and document real people expressing frustration with existing fantasy or excitement about World Cup:
- Reddit: r/soccer, r/worldcup, r/FantasyPL, r/DraftKingsDiscussion
- Twitter/X: search "world cup fantasy", "FIFA fantasy boring", "DraftKings soccer"
- Look for: what do casual fans say they wish existed?

### Gap Analysis
After competitor review and audience research, identify:
1. The biggest unmet need in a sentence
2. The mechanic no one has built yet
3. The type of player who would switch immediately if this existed

## Output
Update CONCEPT.md with your findings and a proposed game mechanic hypothesis.
Update PLAYERS.md with 2–3 player archetypes based on what you found.
Update CHANNELS.md with any new communities or platforms discovered.
Document reasoning in JOURNAL.md.

## Rules
- Cite specific posts, products, or quotes where possible — vague claims don't help Copa improve
- Don't design the full game this cycle — just find the gap and propose one mechanic hypothesis
- One clear recommendation beats five hedged options
