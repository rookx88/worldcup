# Skill: Design

You are designing the game mechanics for a World Cup 2026 daily fantasy game.

## Objective
Develop a complete, playable game design that is more fun, more shareable, and more accessible than anything that currently exists.

## Design Principles
1. **Casual-first** — a fan who watches matches but doesn't track stats should be able to play competitively
2. **Daily format** — tied to the match schedule, not a season-long commitment
3. **Drama over stats** — rewards narrative picks (first scorer, upset, who gets a red card) not just aggregate performance
4. **Instant shareability** — every result should look good as a screenshot or card
5. **Zero barrier** — playable without creating an account for the first round

## Design Tasks

### Core Mechanic
Define clearly:
- What does a player pick each day? (players, outcomes, events, combinations?)
- How are points awarded?
- What makes a pick "bold" vs "safe" — and how are bold picks rewarded differently?
- What is the unique hook in one sentence a 12-year-old can understand?

### Scoring System
Design a scoring system that:
- Rewards correct upset predictions more than chalk picks
- Has a maximum possible score that feels achievable (not just for experts)
- Creates natural drama during the match (when does the score change?)

### Viral Mechanic
Design the sharing moment:
- What does the post-match shareable card look like?
- What triggers someone to share? (best moment: high score, correct upset, winning a friend duel)
- How does a friend join from seeing a shared card?

### Minimum Viable Version
Define what can be cut for v1:
- What is the absolute minimum needed to play one match day?
- What features wait for v2?

## Output
Update CONCEPT.md with the full game design.
Propose anything requiring external action (domain, waitlist tool, landing page) in ACTIONS.md as PENDING.
Document design decisions and tradeoffs in JOURNAL.md.
